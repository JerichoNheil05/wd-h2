Disgust is a basic human emotion characterized by a strong feeling of aversion or repulsion towards certain stimuli, which can be either physical, such as unpleasant smells, tastes, and sights, or psychological, such as immoral behavior. It serves an evolutionary purpose by helping individuals avoid harmful substances and situations that might pose a threat to health and well-being. Disgust is often accompanied by physical reactions like nausea, gagging, or facial expressions of revulsion, and it plays a significant role in social and moral judgments, influencing how people perceive and respond to others' actions.
